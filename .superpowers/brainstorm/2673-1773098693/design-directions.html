A. Studio

Pro creative tool aesthetic. Flat, dark, compact. Inline toolbar controls replace stacked cards. Dropdown pickers for voice and tone. Continuous speed slider. Text area is the hero. Minimal visual layers.

Pros

  • Very compact — no scrolling needed
  • All controls visible at once
  • Speed slider for fine control
  • Focus on the text input

Cons

  • Loses the guided step-by-step feel
  • Emotion grid hidden behind dropdown
  • May feel dense for new users

B. Flow

Single unified card, no steps. Voice, tone, speed, and text all live in one continuous form. Compact pill-style selectors. Tone chips show emoji + label. Settings and text area share one visual container. Generate button anchored at bottom.

Pros

  • Everything in one view — no scrolling
  • Clean, modern, minimal
  • Easy to scan all settings at a glance
  • Familiar form pattern

Cons

  • Tone chips are small — less visual impact
  • Less visual separation between sections
  • May feel cramped with voice design text field

C. Canvas

Split-panel layout with settings + workspace. Left panel holds voice, tone grid, and speed slider. Right panel holds the text editor and a recent generations mini-feed. Tone uses a 2-column grid with emoji icons. Continuous speed slider. All visible without scrolling.

Pros

  • Settings always visible alongside text
  • Recent output gives instant context
  • Tone grid is compact but expressive
  • No scrolling, everything on screen

Cons

  • Needs wider window — may feel cramped at 780pt min
  • Voice Cloning step 1 (drop zone + saved voices) harder to fit
  • More complex layout to maintain
